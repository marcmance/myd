about oaxaca

Located in the south of Mexico and including a long Pacific coast, Oaxaca state has long been neglected by federal authorities but cradled some of the finest thinkers and movements that built the Nation. Oaxaca state has its main roots with the Zapotec and Mixtec civilization, with many diverse languages and cultures across its mountain ranges and valleys of the Sierra Madre Occidental.

Thanks to its colonial architecture and rich flavours of a staple cousine that includes mole, chocolate, coffee and cheese, Oaxaca City (officially named Oaxaca de Juarez) has been a travel hub for European and Australian travelers for many decades. A decidedly independent and strong political stand has created autonomous municipalities around its mountain range, as well as bohemian beach towns along its shore lines.

Around the city
Monte Alban

A sprawling Zapotec ceremonial center on a mountain top overalooking the valley of Oaxaca, Monte Alban was a regional socio-policical and cultural center for a few centuries, peaking in relevance around 900AC. It contains several massive platforms for ceremonial processions, temples, palaces and a ball court.

Mitla

Archeological site near Oaxaca city featuring exquisite and elaborate stone carved mosaics of patterns and geometric designs over tombs, panels and walls, carefully cut and polished to be assembled without the use of mortar. Constructions were more for lush residential purposes than the ceremonial and political relevance of Monte Alban.

Teotitlan del Valle

A small village near the city best known for its textiles, woven rugs and hand operated looms. Wool is obtained from sheep and dyed with natural pigments made of insects, bush, wild plants or trees. Patterns and specific dye colors are often specialty of the particular shop and passed on through generations of family artisan solos.

Arbor del Tule

Ahuehuete tree on the grounds of a church on the way to Mitla, with a turnk measuring 140ft around and 46ft diameter, it has the stoutest trunk in the world! It took DNA testing to determine it to be a single tree and thought to be about 1,500 years old. Its crown provides enough shade for our entire wedding party to drink mezcal for hours and is still a potential site for the reception as long as I can set up turntables on a tree branch.

Hierve el Agua

A set of natural rock formations, grown over thousands of years by natural springs heavy on minerals. Two primary formations are almost 300ft tall and could be easily mistaken as a frozen salt waterfall. Site also features natural spring swimming pools and guided hiking around the cliffs and jagged paths.
